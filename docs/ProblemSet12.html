Ans1: This is simply regression to the mean of the performance variable. Performance on the first maneuver is not perfectly correlated to the performance on the second maneuver after positive reinforcement. So Pilots who did the first maneuver pretty well might just regress to their average expected performance in the 2nd maneuver. This would make their performance look worse off than the last try. The pilots who didn’t do the first maneuver well might also regress to the mean performance. This would make their performance look better relatively. Regression explains this phenomenon well.

Ans2: a) Coefficient of determination or proportion of variance explained is square of correlation coefficient. We can also find it out using regression line.

sister<-c(69, 64, 65, 63, 65, 62, 65, 64, 66, 59, 62)     
brother<- c(71, 68, 66, 67, 70, 71, 70, 73, 72, 65, 66)
n<-length(sister)
slope<- cor(sister,brother)* (sd(brother)/sd(sister))
intercept<- mean(brother) - (slope*mean(sister))
yhat<- slope*sister+intercept
errors<-yhat-brother
final_df<-data.frame(cbind(sister,brother,yhat,errors))
proporation_variance_unexplained<- var(errors)/var(brother)
proporation_variance_explained<- 1-(var(errors)/var(brother))
proporation_variance_explained
## [1] 0.3114251
(cor(sister,brother))^2
## [1] 0.3114251

We can see that ‘proporation_variance_explained’ and correlation coefficient square are the same.
